**Short answer:** **Yes, you can retake AP exams** — once per subject per year in May. All scores are retained unless canceled; most colleges consider your best performance.

## How AP Retakes Work

1. Register through your school (or authorized test center) for the next May administration.
2. Take the full exam again — MCQ and FRQ together.
3. Receive a new score in July alongside other AP results.

You cannot retake mid-year or split sections.

## Score Reporting

- By default, all AP scores are sent to colleges you designate.
- You may **withhold** or **cancel** a score through College Board if you do not want a specific result reported.
- Many admissions readers focus on your **best** AP score per subject when multiple years appear.

## When a Retake Makes Sense

| Situation | Retake? |
|---|---|
| Scored 2, need 3+ for credit | Often yes |
| Scored 3, target school wants 4+ for major | Maybe — check policy |
| Scored 4, wanted 5 for pride | Usually no |
| Missed exam day (illness) | Yes — official makeup via school |
