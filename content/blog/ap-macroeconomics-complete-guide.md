The 2026 AP Macroeconomics exam is **Friday, May 8, 2026 (12 PM local)**.

## Exam Structure

Hybrid digital — **2 hours 10 minutes**. 60 MCQs (~67%) + 3 FRQs (~33%).

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Basic Economic Concepts | 5–10% |
| 2 | Economic Indicators and Business Cycle | 12–17% |
| 3 | National Income and Price Determination | 17–27% |
| 4 | Financial Sector | 15–23% |
| 5 | Long-Run Consequences of Stabilization Policies | 20–30% |
| 6 | Open Economy — Trade and Finance | 10–13% |

**Units 3 + 5 = ~37–57%** — AD-AS, fiscal policy, and Phillips curve dominate.

## FRQ Archetypes

1. **Long FRQ** — multi-part AD-AS or Phillips curve analysis (10 pts).
2. **Short FRQ** — money market or loanable funds graph (5 pts).
3. **Short FRQ** — international trade or forex (5 pts).

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Units 2–3 (GDP, unemployment, inflation, AD-AS).

**Weeks 3–4:** Units 4–5 (money market, fiscal/monetary policy, Phillips curve).

**Week 5:** Unit 6 (trade, balance of payments).

**Week 6:** Released FRQs + [Macro score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-macroeconomics).
