AP Microeconomics and AP Macroeconomics are often confused. Here's how they differ — and how to choose.

## Quick Comparison

| | AP Micro | AP Macro |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Individual markets, firms, consumers | Whole economy: GDP, inflation, policy |
| Key graphs | Supply/demand, cost curves, market structures | AD-AS, Phillips curve, money market |
| Heaviest units | Supply/Demand + Imperfect Competition | AD-AS + Stabilization Policies |
| Pass rate (2025) | ~68% | ~67% |
| Exam date 2026 | May 4 (12 PM) | May 8 (12 PM) |

## AP Microeconomics

Studies **how individual agents make decisions**: why prices rise, how monopolies behave, when markets fail.

- 60 MCQs + 3 FRQs (10+5+5 points)
- Graph-heavy — label every axis and curve

[Micro complete guide](/blog/ap-microeconomics-complete-guide) · [Score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-microeconomics)

## AP Macroeconomics

Studies **the economy as a whole**: unemployment, inflation, fiscal and monetary policy, international trade.

- Same exam structure as Micro
- AD-AS model is the backbone of the course

[Macro complete guide](/blog/ap-macroeconomics-complete-guide) · [Score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-macroeconomics)

## Can You Take Both?

Yes — and many students do in the same year since exam dates don't conflict (May 4 vs May 8). The graph-drawing skills transfer between courses.
