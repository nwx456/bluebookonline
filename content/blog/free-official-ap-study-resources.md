Before buying a $50 prep book, know this: **College Board provides extensive free official materials** for every AP course. Used correctly, they're more accurate than third-party content because they're written by the same people who build the exam.

This guide maps every free official resource — and shows where [AP Practice Exam Online](/exams) supplements gaps with additional Bluebook-style practice.

## 1. Course and Exam Description (CED)

**What:** The official syllabus — units, topics, exam format, sample questions  
**Where:** [AP Central → Courses](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses) → select your subject → "Course and Exam Description"  
**Cost:** Free PDF  
**Best for:** Understanding unit weights, exam structure, and what "college-level" means for each topic

**Action:** Download your CED first. Build your [self-study plan](/blog/how-to-self-study-ap-exams) around unit percentages.

## 2. AP Daily Videos

**What:** Short video lessons covering every unit, taught by AP teachers  
**Where:** [AP Daily on AP Central](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/instructional-resources/ap-daily) or YouTube  
**Cost:** Free  
**Best for:** Learning content when you don't have a classroom teacher

**Tip:** Watch at 1.25x speed during review weeks. Pair each video with 5 MCQ from [practice tests](/exams).

## 3. AP Classroom

**What:** Digital platform with **Progress Checks** (unit quizzes) and **Practice Exams**  
**Where:** [myap.collegeboard.org](https://myap.collegeboard.org) — requires teacher unlock  
**Cost:** Free (through school enrollment)  
**Best for:** Authentic College Board question style and full-length practice exams

**Self-studiers:** Ask your school's AP coordinator if they can enroll you in AP Classroom for your self-study subject. Many coordinators will help.

## 4. Bluebook Practice Exams

**What:** Full digital practice exams in the actual testing app  
**Where:** [Bluebook app](https://bluebook.app.collegeboard.org)  
**Cost:** Free  
**Best for:** Rehearsing the exact digital interface before exam day

**Also read:** [Bluebook interface explained](/blog/bluebook-interface-explained) · [Digital vs hybrid formats](/blog/fully-digital-vs-hybrid-ap-exams)

## 5. Past Free-Response Questions

**What:** Previous years' FRQs with **scoring guidelines** and sample responses  
**Where:** AP Central → your course → "Exam" section → Free-Response Questions  
**Cost:** Free  
**Best for:** FRQ practice — the highest-ROI activity for essay and science exams

**Digital exams:** Practice typing responses. Use our [FRQ module](/dashboard/upload?kind=frq) for AI rubric feedback between official sessions.

## 6. AP Daily: Practice Sessions

**What:** Live and recorded sessions walking through practice problems  
**Where:** AP Central instructional resources  
**Cost:** Free  
**Best for:** Spring review leading up to May exams

## 7. Score Distribution Reports

**What:** Annual data on what % of students earned each score  
**Where:** [AP Score Reports](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/score-reports/annual)  
**Cost:** Free  
**Best for:** Setting realistic goals — see our [2026 distributions guide](/blog/ap-score-distributions)
