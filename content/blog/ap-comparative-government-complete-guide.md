The 2026 AP Comparative Government exam is **Wednesday, May 6, 2026 (12 PM local)**.

## Exam Structure

Fully digital — **2 hours 30 minutes**.

| Section | Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section I (MCQ) | 55 | 50% |
| Section II (FRQ) | 4 | 50% |

### Four FRQs

| FRQ | Task | Points |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conceptual Analysis | 3 |
| 2 | Quantitative Analysis | 5 |
| 3 | Comparative Analysis | 4 |
| 4 | Argument Essay | 5 |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Political Systems, Regimes, Governments | 18–22% |
| 2 | Political Institutions | 17–23% |
| 3 | Political Culture and Participation | 17–23% |
| 4 | Party and Electoral Systems | 12–18% |
| 5 | Political and Economic Change | 17–23% |

## Six-Country Comparison Chart

Build a master chart comparing: **regime type, head of state/government, legislature, judiciary, civil society, economic system** for all six countries. This chart alone prepares you for half the exam.

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Regime types + institutions framework.

**Weeks 3–4:** Deep dive two countries per week (China/Iran, then Mexico/Nigeria, then Russia/UK).

**Weeks 5–6:** FRQ practice — comparative analysis is the highest-value skill.
