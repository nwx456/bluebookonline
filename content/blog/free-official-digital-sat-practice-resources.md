The best SAT prep is **free and official**. The College Board provides more no-cost resources than most students use — this guide maps every free tool and how to fit them into your study plan.

> **Related:** [Complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-complete-guide) · [One-month plan](/blog/digital-sat-one-month-study-plan) · [Score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator)

## 1. Bluebook App (Highest Priority)

**What it is:** The official testing application — the same software used on test day.

**What's free:**
- Full-length adaptive practice tests
- Same interface, timing, and tools as the real exam (Desmos, annotate, grid-in)

**How to use it:**
1. Download from [bluebook.app.collegeboard.org](https://bluebook.app.collegeboard.org)
2. Complete **Practice Test 1** under timed conditions in your first week of prep
3. Enter module scores in the [SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator)
4. Save additional tests for weeks 3–4 of your study plan

**Why it matters:** No third-party platform replicates adaptive Module 2 routing. Bluebook is non-negotiable.

## 2. SAT Question Bank

**What it is:** Filterable practice questions on [satsuite.collegeboard.org](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org).

**What's free:**
- Hundreds of official questions
- Filter by skill domain, difficulty, and question type (including grid-in)

**How to use it:**
1. After a practice test, identify your weakest domain (e.g., Standard English Conventions)
2. Filter Question Bank to that domain
3. Complete 20–30 questions untimed, then 20 timed
4. Review every miss — official explanations are included

## 3. Khan Academy SAT

**What it is:** Free skill-based SAT prep at [khanacademy.org/sat](https://www.khanacademy.org/sat).

**What's free:**
- Video lessons by skill
- Practice drills linked to College Board content
- Progress tracking

**How to use it:**
1. Link your College Board account for personalized recommendations
2. Use for **content learning** (grammar rules, algebra concepts)
3. Do not rely on Khan alone for full-test pacing — pair with Bluebook

## 4. Official SAT Study Guide (PDF)

**What it is:** Sample questions and test overview published by the College Board.

**How to use it:** Supplementary reading for format understanding. The Bluebook app is more useful for practice.

## 5. AP Practice Exam Online (Unofficial but Free)

**What it is:** Free Bluebook-style SAT practice on this platform.

**How to use it:**
- Additional full-length tests beyond Bluebook's limited free set
- Build pacing and stamina between official tests
- Practice in the same interface style as test day

## What to Avoid

- **Random PDFs from unofficial sites** — may not match current Digital SAT format
- **Only using third-party tests** — adaptive experience requires Bluebook
- **Cramming Question Bank without reviewing misses** — quality review beats quantity
