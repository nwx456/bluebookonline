The DBQ is **25% of your AP European History score** — the single highest-weighted individual component. Official rubric: [AP Euro DBQ scoring guidelines](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-european-history/exam) on AP Central.

## 7-Point Rubric Breakdown

| Point | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Thesis** — historically defensible claim that establishes a line of reasoning |
| 2 | **Contextualization** — broader historical context beyond the prompt |
| 3 | **Evidence from documents** — use content from ≥3 documents to support argument |
| 4 | **Evidence from documents (continued)** — additional document evidence |
| 5 | **Sourcing (HIPP/HTS)** — explain POV, purpose, audience, or context of ≥2 documents |
| 6 | **Outside evidence** — specific example not found in any document |
| 7 | **Complexity** — nuanced argument showing multiple perspectives or causation |

## Step-by-Step Strategy

### Minutes 0–10: Read and Annotate

- Read the prompt twice. Underline the task verb (evaluate, explain, compare).
- Skim all 7 documents. Label each: **S** (support), **C** (challenge), **N** (neutral).
- Note author, date, and audience for sourcing points.

### Minutes 10–15: Plan

- Draft thesis: "Between [dates], [change/continuity] occurred because..."
- Pick 4 documents (minimum 3 for evidence, 2 for sourcing).
- Identify one outside evidence example from your course content.

### Minutes 15–55: Write

**Introduction (1 paragraph):** Thesis + contextualization.

**Body (2–3 paragraphs):** Group documents by argument, not by document number. Integrate sourcing: "As a [author's role], Document 3 reveals..."

**Outside evidence paragraph:** "Furthermore, [specific event/example] demonstrates..."

### Minutes 55–60: Review

Check: thesis present? 3+ documents cited? 2+ sourced? Outside evidence included?

## Common Mistakes

1. **Summarizing documents** instead of using them as evidence
2. **Missing sourcing** — POV/purpose/audience must be explicit
3. **No outside evidence** — you need at least one example from your knowledge
4. **Prompt drift** — answer the specific question asked
