One month is enough time for meaningful SAT improvement — if you spend it on the right activities. This plan assumes **6–10 hours per week** and access to free official resources.

> **Related:** [Free practice resources](/blog/free-official-digital-sat-practice-resources) · [Exam day checklist](/blog/digital-sat-exam-day-checklist) · [Score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator)

## Before You Start

1. Download the **Bluebook app**
2. Pick your [test date](/blog/sat-test-dates-2026)
3. Block study time on your calendar (3 sessions/week minimum)

## Week 1: Diagnostic and Baseline

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Bluebook Practice Test 1 (full, timed) | 2.5 hr |
| Tue | Score with [calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator); categorize misses by domain | 1 hr |
| Wed | R&W: drill weakest domain via Question Bank (20 Q) | 45 min |
| Thu | Math: drill weakest domain via Question Bank (20 Q) | 45 min |
| Fri | Read [adaptive testing guide](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works) | 30 min |
| Sat | Khan Academy: 2 skill lessons in weak areas | 1 hr |
| Sun | Rest or light vocab review | 20 min |

**Week 1 goal:** Know your baseline score and top 2 weak domains.

## Week 2: Content Deep Dive

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon–Tue | R&W weak domain: 40 Question Bank items + review all misses | 1.5 hr |
| Wed–Thu | Math weak domain: 40 Question Bank items + [Desmos practice](/blog/desmos-sat-math-tips) | 1.5 hr |
| Fri | [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy): timed Module 1 only from Practice Test 1 | 1 hr |
| Sat | [Grid-in drill](/blog/sat-grid-in-student-produced-response-guide): 15 questions | 45 min |
| Sun | Review error log from Week 1 | 30 min |

**Week 2 goal:** Close content gaps in your two weakest domains.

## Week 3: Full Test and Refinement

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Bluebook Practice Test 2 (full, timed) | 2.5 hr |
| Tue | Score and compare to Week 1 — did weak domains improve? | 1 hr |
| Wed | R&W: 30 timed questions in previously weak domain | 45 min |
| Thu | Math: 30 timed questions + 5 grid-ins | 45 min |
| Fri | Full test on [AP Practice Exam Online](/exams?program=sat) | 2.5 hr |
| Sat | Review all misses from Practice Test 2 | 1 hr |
| Sun | Read [Bluebook interface guide](/blog/bluebook-sat-interface-explained) | 30 min |

**Week 3 goal:** Confirm score improvement with a second official practice test.

## Week 4: Final Push and Taper

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon–Tue | Targeted drill: only question types you still miss | 1 hr |
| Wed | Bluebook Practice Test 3 (full, timed) — final scored test | 2.5 hr |
| Thu | Light review: error log, [Module 1 checklist](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) | 45 min |
| Fri | [Exam day checklist](/blog/digital-sat-exam-day-checklist) — download Bluebook, check ID | 30 min |
| Sat | 20 easy questions per section (confidence building) | 40 min |
| Sun | **Rest.** No studying. Sleep well. | — |

**Week 4 goal:** Peak performance on test day — not new learning.

## Expected Improvement

| Starting Score | Realistic 1-Month Gain |
|---|---|
| 1000–1100 | 80–120 points |
| 1100–1200 | 60–100 points |
| 1200–1300 | 40–80 points |
| 1300–1400 | 20–50 points |
| 1400+ | 10–30 points |

See [improve 100 points](/blog/how-to-improve-sat-score-100-points) for retake-specific tactics.

## If You Have Less Than One Month

- Skip Week 2 content deep dive — go straight to timed Question Bank drills
- Take only 2 full tests (Week 1 and Week 3)
- Focus 70% of time on your single weakest section

## If You Have More Than One Month

- Add a fourth full test in Week 5–6
- Spread content review across Weeks 2–3 instead of cramming
- Consider a second month focused on [superscore retake](/blog/sat-superscore-explained)
