A **100-point SAT improvement** is one of the most achievable retake goals — yet most students repeat the same prep and get the same score. This playbook focuses on the three levers that actually move scores: **weakness diagnosis**, **section-specific drills**, and **Module 1 accuracy**.

> **Related:** [Superscore guide](/blog/sat-superscore-explained) · [One-month plan](/blog/digital-sat-one-month-study-plan) · [Score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator)

## Step 1: Diagnose (Not Just "Study More")

After your first SAT, do not start generic prep. Instead:

1. Enter module scores in the [SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator)
2. List every missed question by **skill domain** (use Bluebook's score report or Question Bank categories)
3. Identify your **#1 weakness** — the domain where you lost the most points

Example diagnosis:

| Domain | Misses | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Standard English Conventions | 8 | **#1 — fix this** |
| Advanced Math | 5 | #2 |
| Craft and Structure | 3 | #3 |
| Geometry | 2 | Low priority |

## Step 2: The 60/40 Rule

Spend **60% of prep time** on your weakest section, **40%** maintaining your stronger one.

If R&W is weak and Math is strong:
- 4 R&W sessions per week, 2 Math maintenance sessions
- Do not ignore Math entirely — a 50-point Math drop erases R&W gains

## Step 3: Section-Specific Tactics

### For R&W Gains (+50–80 points)

- **Grammar/conventions** are the fastest points: 40 Question Bank items in Standard English Conventions, review every miss
- **Stop over-reading passages** — one question per passage means targeted reading
- **Module 1 accuracy** — see [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy)

### For Math Gains (+50–80 points)

- **Algebra and Advanced Math = 70% of Math** — master linear equations and quadratics first
- **Use Desmos on every graphing question** — see [Desmos tips](/blog/desmos-sat-math-tips)
- **Grid-in practice** — 15 grid-ins/week; never leave blank ([grid-in guide](/blog/sat-grid-in-student-produced-response-guide))

## Step 4: Module 1 Focus

Many retake students lose points not from content gaps but from **easy Module 1 mistakes** that cap their adaptive path.

Before your retake:
- Take 3 timed Module 1-only sessions (R&W and Math separately)
- Target 20+/27 R&W and 16+/22 Math in Module 1
- Read [adaptive testing explained](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works)

## Step 5: Superscore Strategy

If your colleges superscore:

| First sitting | Retake target | Superscore |
|---|---|---|
| R&W 580, Math 720 (1300) | R&W 660+, maintain Math 700+ | **1420+** |

You only need to improve one section significantly. See [superscore explained](/blog/sat-superscore-explained).

## Step 6: Full Test Cadence

| Week | Activity |
|---|---|
| 1 | Diagnostic review + domain drills |
| 2–3 | Targeted Question Bank (40 Q/day weak domain) |
| 4 | Full Bluebook practice test |
| 5 | Second full test + error log review |
| 6 | Retake |

Follow the detailed [one-month plan](/blog/digital-sat-one-month-study-plan) if you have exactly 4 weeks.

## What NOT to Do

- **Retake within 2 weeks** — not enough time for meaningful improvement
- **Only take third-party tests** — adaptive experience requires Bluebook
- **Study new content the night before** — trust your preparation
- **Ignore Module 1** — routing caps scores regardless of content knowledge

## Realistic Timelines

| Starting Score | 100-Point Gain Timeline |
|---|---|
| 1050–1150 | 4–6 weeks with focused prep |
| 1150–1250 | 6–8 weeks |
| 1250–1350 | 8–10 weeks (harder — fewer "easy" points left) |
| 1350+ | 100 points is very difficult; aim for 30–50 |

Track progress with the [score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) after each practice test. When your practice scores consistently exceed your target, schedule your retake using [2026 test dates](/blog/sat-test-dates-2026).
