**Short answer:** Digital SAT Math is 70 minutes — two adaptive modules of 22 questions each, Desmos calculator allowed throughout, ~25% grid-in.

Digital SAT **Math** contributes 200–800 points toward your total score. The section includes multiple-choice and **student-produced response** (grid-in) questions, with a **Desmos graphing calculator** available for every question.

This guide covers content weights, Desmos tactics, grid-in rules, and Module 1 strategy for 2026.

> **Related:** [Digital SAT complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-complete-guide) · [Desmos SAT tips](/blog/desmos-sat-math-tips) · [Grid-in guide](/blog/sat-grid-in-student-produced-response-guide)

## Section Format

| Module | Questions | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Module 1 | 22 | 35 min |
| Module 2 | 22 | 35 min |

Average pacing: **~95 seconds per question**, but grid-in and multi-step algebra need more time.

## Content Domains (Approximate Weights)

| Domain | Weight | Example topics |
|---|---|---|
| Algebra | ~35% | Linear equations, systems, inequalities |
| Advanced Math | ~35% | Quadratics, exponentials, polynomials |
| Problem-Solving & Data Analysis | ~15% | Ratios, percentages, statistics |
| Geometry & Trigonometry | ~15% | Area, volume, right-triangle trig |

**Algebra + Advanced Math = ~70% of Math.** If you have limited study time, prioritize linear and quadratic fluency.

## Multiple-Choice Strategy

1. **Plug in answer choices** when solving equations — start with (B) or (C) on medium questions.
2. **Use Desmos for systems and intersections** — graph both equations and read the intersection point.
3. **Mark and return** on word problems that require setup; knock out quick algebra first.

Deep Desmos tactics: [Desmos SAT Math tips](/blog/desmos-sat-math-tips).

## Student-Produced Response (Grid-In)

About **11 grid-in questions** appear across both modules (~25% of Math). Key rules:

- Enter up to **5 characters** (including decimal point or fraction slash).
- **No negative signs** — if your answer is negative, you made an error.
- Fractions can be entered as `3/4` or decimal `0.75`.
- **Multiple correct forms** may be accepted (e.g., `2` and `2.0`).

Full rules and strategy: [SAT grid-in guide](/blog/sat-grid-in-student-produced-response-guide).

## Desmos on Test Day

Bluebook's built-in Desmos is the same engine students use in class. High-value uses:

- **Graph systems of equations** — find intersection for `(x, y)` answers
- **Regression for scatterplots** — fit a line or curve to data points
- **Inequalities** — shade regions to verify solution sets

You do not need to master every Desmos feature — focus on graphing, intersections, and table values.

## Module 1 Math Strategy

Math Module 1 routing works like R&W:

1. Secure easy algebra and data questions first.
2. Do not leave grid-ins blank — enter your best estimate.
3. Strong Module 1 performance unlocks the hard Module 2 path needed for 700+ Math.

See [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) and [adaptive testing explained](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works).

## Common Mistakes

- **Not using Desmos** on questions where graphing saves 60+ seconds.
- **Misreading "which value of x"** when the question asks for `x` but choices show expressions.
- **Grid-in format errors** — entering `1/3` when the answer must be a decimal, or exceeding 5 characters.
