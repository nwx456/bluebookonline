It's April. Your AP exam is in 3–4 weeks. You're not alone — r/APStudents enters full panic mode every spring. A structured **1-month cram plan** beats random chapter re-reading.

This schedule assumes you've covered most content once and need **consolidation + exam skills**. If you're starting from zero, prioritize [self-study fundamentals](/blog/how-to-self-study-ap-exams) and consider whether a May exam is realistic.

## Before Week 1: Setup (1 Day)

- [ ] Download CED; highlight **top 3 heaviest units**
- [ ] Install Bluebook; run device check
- [ ] Take one **diagnostic** [practice test](/exams) — no timing pressure, just baseline
- [ ] Enter results in [score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator) — identify weak section (MCQ vs FRQ)
- [ ] Confirm exam format: [digital vs hybrid](/blog/fully-digital-vs-hybrid-ap-exams)

## Week 1: High-Weight Units + MCQ Foundation

**Goal:** Close content gaps in the units worth the most exam points.

| Day | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon–Tue | CED Units 1–2 (or your heaviest units) + AP Daily videos | 2 hrs/day |
| Wed–Thu | CED Units 3–4 + 20 timed MCQ | 2 hrs/day |
| Fri | 30 timed MCQ from [practice tests](/exams) | 2 hrs |
| Sat | Review all wrong answers in [Mistake Review](/dashboard/mistakes) | 2 hrs |
| Sun | Rest or light flashcard review | 1 hr |

**Output:** ~100 MCQ attempted; weak topics identified.

## Week 2: FRQ Focus + More MCQ

**Goal:** FRQs are 33–60% of your score — start practicing now.

| Day | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Review 2 past FRQs with scoring guidelines (AP Central) | 2 hrs |
| Tue | Write 1 timed FRQ (typed or handwritten per your format) | 1.5 hrs |
| Wed | 30 timed MCQ | 1.5 hrs |
| Thu | [FRQ practice](/dashboard/upload?kind=frq) or past FRQ #2 | 2 hrs |
| Fri | CED remaining high-weight units | 2 hrs |
| Sat | **Half-length timed exam** (MCQ only or MCQ + 1 FRQ) | 3 hrs |
| Sun | Score + review mistakes | 2 hrs |

**Digital FRQ tips:** [Typing strategies](/blog/digital-frq-typing-strategies)  
**Hybrid FRQ tips:** [Paper booklet guide](/blog/hybrid-ap-exam-frq-booklets)

## Week 3: Full Timed Exams + Targeted Repair

**Goal:** Simulate exam day twice this week.

| Day | Task | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | **Full timed practice exam #1** on [AP Practice Exam Online](/exams) | 3+ hrs |
| Tue | Deep review every missed question | 2 hrs |
| Wed | [Generate custom quiz](/dashboard/generate) on weakest unit (15 Q) | 1.5 hrs |
| Thu | **Full timed practice exam #2** (different test) | 3+ hrs |
| Fri | FRQ rubric study — read sample 5-scoring responses | 2 hrs |
| Sat | Calculator/reference panel practice (if applicable) | 1 hr |
| Sun | Light review; organize [exam day checklist](/blog/ap-exam-day-checklist) | 1 hr |

Track composite scores in [Insights](/dashboard/insights) — you want upward trend even if absolute score isn't a 5 yet.

## Week 4: Exam Week Taper

**Goal:** Maintain readiness without burnout.

| Days before exam | Activity |
|---|---|
| 7–5 days out | One final half-exam; review formula sheet / key terms |
| 4–3 days out | FRQ outline practice (thesis + evidence bullets, not full essays) |
| 2 days out | Read [how AP scoring works](/blog/how-ap-exams-are-scored); visualize section strategy |
| 1 day out | Pack bag, charge device, sleep 8 hours — **no cramming past 8 PM** |
| Exam day | Execute [exam day checklist](/blog/ap-exam-day-checklist) |

## Subject-Specific Cram Adjustments

| Subject | Cram priority |
|---|---|
| AP Calculus AB/BC | Calculator vs non-calculator sections; [Calc calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-calculus-ab) |
| AP Bio / Chem | Lab FRQ vocabulary; process diagrams |
| APUSH / World | DBQ thesis formula; one timed DBQ this week |
| AP Lang | Rhetorical analysis device list; one essay per type |
| AP Psych | Term-definition pairs; MCQ is 67% — drill MCQ hard |
| AP CSA | Code by question type; Java Quick Reference familiarity |

## When One Month Isn't Enough

If your diagnostic scored below 40% composite:

- Focus on earning a **3** ([what a 3 means](/blog/is-a-3-on-ap-exam-good))
- Prioritize MCQ (guessing has no penalty)
- Consider whether late testing or next year is better
