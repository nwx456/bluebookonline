Test day surprises cause more score drops than hard questions. This checklist covers everything to prepare **1 week before**, **the night before**, and **morning of** your Digital SAT.

> **Related:** [Bluebook interface](/blog/bluebook-sat-interface-explained) · [Test dates 2026](/blog/sat-test-dates-2026) · [Complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-complete-guide)

## One Week Before

- [ ] Confirm your test center location and arrival time on your College Board account
- [ ] Verify your **photo ID** is valid and name matches registration exactly
- [ ] Download **Bluebook** on your testing device from [bluebook.app.collegeboard.org](https://bluebook.app.collegeboard.org)
- [ ] Log in to Bluebook with your College Board credentials
- [ ] Run the **Exam Setup** check in Bluebook (Wi-Fi, storage, battery)
- [ ] Charge your device fully and locate your charger
- [ ] Take a final timed practice test if you have not tested in 5+ days

## The Night Before

- [ ] Pack your bag (see "What to Bring" below)
- [ ] Set two alarms
- [ ] **No cramming** — review your [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) notes only (10 min max)
- [ ] Sleep 8+ hours — sleep deprivation hurts Module 1 accuracy more than any content gap
- [ ] Confirm Bluebook shows your upcoming test on the home screen

## What to Bring

| Item | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Valid photo ID | **Yes** | Driver's license, passport, or school ID with photo |
| Admission ticket | **Yes** | Printed or on phone — from College Board account |
| Testing device | **Yes** | Laptop or tablet approved by your test center |
| Charger | Recommended | Some centers have outlets; bring yours |
| Approved calculator | Optional | Desmos is built into Bluebook for all Math |
| Pencils | Optional | For scratch work if center allows paper |
| Snacks and water | Recommended | For the 10-minute break only |
| Watch | No | Timer is built into Bluebook |

## What NOT to Bring

- Phone (must be off and stored during the entire test)
- Smartwatch or fitness tracker
- Notes, books, or formula sheets
- Highlighters (Bluebook has digital highlight tools)

## Morning Of

- [ ] Eat a normal breakfast — do not skip or try new foods
- [ ] Arrive **30 minutes early**
- [ ] Bathroom before check-in
- [ ] Turn phone **completely off** before entering the testing room
- [ ] Open Bluebook and confirm your test appears
- [ ] Take 3 deep breaths before starting Module 1

## During the Test

### Reading & Writing (64 minutes total)
- Two modules of 32 minutes each
- Use **mark-for-review** liberally — see [Bluebook interface guide](/blog/bluebook-sat-interface-explained)
- Hide the timer for the first half of each module if it helps anxiety

### Break (10 minutes)
- Leave the testing room if allowed
- Eat your snack, use the bathroom
- **Do not open your phone**
- Do not discuss specific questions with other students

### Math (70 minutes total)
- Two modules of 35 minutes each
- **Desmos is available for every question** — use it
- Enter all grid-in answers — never leave blank

## After the Test

- Scores release in **2–3 weeks** on your College Board account
- Enter module scores in the [SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) if Bluebook provides a breakdown
- If retaking, check [superscore policy](/blog/sat-superscore-explained) at your target colleges

## Device Troubleshooting

If Bluebook crashes during the test:

1. **Do not panic** — proctors are trained for this
2. Raise your hand immediately
3. Your answers are auto-saved — you will resume where you left off
4. Report the issue to the proctor for a potential irregularity form

Good luck — you prepared for this. Trust your [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) and move on from hard questions without dwelling.
