"I can take any AP next year — which ones?" Guidance counselors give general advice; Reddit gives conflicting opinions. This **grade-by-grade roadmap** balances College Board data, typical school sequences, and college admissions reality.

Cross-reference [hardest and easiest AP classes](/blog/hardest-easiest-ap-classes) and [how many APs for top colleges](/blog/how-many-ap-classes) as you plan.

## Freshman Year (Grade 9): Foundation

**Typical AP load:** 0–1

| Good options | Why |
|---|---|
| AP Human Geography | Intro-level; builds study habits |
| AP Computer Science Principles | Broad exposure; no deep coding |

**Avoid:** Loading multiple APs before you've learned time management.

**Prep:** If taking HUG, start [practice tests](/exams/ap-human-geography) by March.

## Sophomore Year (Grade 10): Explore Interests

**Typical AP load:** 1–3

| Track | Suggested APs |
|---|---|
| STEM interest | AP CSA or AP Precalc (if ready), AP Physics 1 (if strong in math) |
| Humanities | AP World History, AP Psych |
| Well-rounded | AP Psych + AP World or AP Euro |

**Key decision:** Are you on track for Calculus senior year? If yes, plan toward [Calc AB vs BC](/blog/ap-calculus-ab-vs-bc).

**Self-study option:** AP Psych and AP Micro are popular [self-study picks](/blog/easiest-ap-exams-to-self-study).

## Junior Year (Grade 11): Peak Rigor

**Typical AP load:** 3–5 (most competitive applicants peak here)

| Track | Core APs |
|---|---|
| STEM / Engineering | AP Calc AB or BC, AP Physics 1 or C, AP Chem, AP CSA |
| Pre-med / Bio | AP Bio, AP Chem, AP Calc AB, AP Psych |
| Humanities / Law | APUSH, AP Lang, AP Gov, AP Euro |
| Business / Econ | AP Micro, AP Macro, AP Stats, AP Calc AB |

**Critical:** Junior year grades matter most for admissions. Don't take 5 APs if you'll earn Bs/Cs.

**Exam prep:** Use [free official resources](/blog/free-official-ap-study-resources) starting January; [1-month cram plan](/blog/one-month-ap-study-plan) in April.

## Senior Year (Grade 12): Depth + Credit

**Typical AP load:** 2–4 (often lighter than junior year)

Senior APs serve two purposes:
1. **College credit** — scores sent after admission
2. **Major preparation** — AP Calc BC before engineering, AP Gov before poli sci

| Strategy | Example schedule |
|---|---|
| Double down on major | Future CS major: AP CSA + AP Calc BC |
| Clean up gaps | Didn't take APUSH? Take it senior year |
| Light load for apps | 2 APs + college applications in fall |

Scores still release [July 6](/blog/when-do-ap-scores-come-out) — after most admission decisions. Senior APs primarily affect **credit**, not admission.

## Decision Framework: 5 Questions

1. **What does my school offer?** — Can't take AP Bio if it's not offered
2. **What are prerequisites?** — Calc before Physics C; Alg 2 before Stats
3. **What's my weekly study budget?** — Each AP needs 4–6+ hrs/week near May
4. **What's my target major?** — Align STEM APs for STEM programs
5. **What's my realistic score target?** — Check [pass rates](/blog/ap-score-distributions)

## Digital Exam Format Considerations
