The Digital SAT uses **multistage adaptive testing (MST)** — the most debated topic on r/SAT every testing season. This guide explains exactly how Module 2 routing works, why it creates a "score ceiling," and what you should do about it.

> **Related:** [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) · [Score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) · [Complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-complete-guide)

## How MST Works on the Digital SAT

Each scored section has two modules:

```
Module 1 (fixed difficulty for all students)
        ↓
   Performance evaluated
        ↓
Module 2a (easier)  OR  Module 2b (harder)
```

**Reading & Writing** and **Math** adapt independently. You could get hard R&W + easy Math, or any combination.

## What Changes Between Paths

| Factor | Easy Module 2 | Hard Module 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Question difficulty | Lower | Higher |
| Score ceiling | ~650–700 per section | 750–800 per section |
| Item pool | Fewer top-difficulty items | Full difficulty range |

The College Board does not publish exact routing thresholds. Based on practice test data and student reports, **roughly 60–70% correct in Module 1** often qualifies for the hard path — but this varies by form.

## Why Module 1 Is the Highest-Leverage Section

Students who cruise through Module 1 and miss easy questions often get routed to the easy Module 2 path — **permanently capping their section score** for that test.

Example: A student who gets 22/27 on R&W Module 1 but misses five medium questions they could have answered may land on the easy path and max out around 680 R&W — even with a perfect Module 2.

This is why [Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy) focuses on **accuracy over speed**.

## Score Ceiling: The Reddit Question

The recurring r/SAT post: *"I got 25/27 on Module 1 but only scored 650 — why?"*

Possible explanations:

1. **Easy Module 2 path** — despite strong Module 1, routing algorithms consider item-level performance, not just raw counts.
2. **Hard Module 2 with misses** — you got the hard path but missed several high-value items.
3. **Equating differences** — practice test conversions differ from live test equating.

## Does Adaptive Testing Make Prep Harder?

Not really — it makes **Module 1 prep more important**. The content tested is identical across paths; only difficulty distribution changes.

Focus areas:

1. **Secure easy and medium questions in Module 1** — grammar, basic algebra, straightforward data.
2. **Do not leave Module 1 blanks** — guessing is free (no penalty).
3. **Practice with Bluebook** — the only way to experience real adaptive routing.

## Adaptive Testing vs Old SAT

The paper SAT was linear — every student saw the same questions in the same order. The Digital SAT's MST means two students with the same total raw score can have different scaled scores if they took different Module 2 paths.
