"What's the easiest AP to self-study?" is one of the most searched questions on r/APStudents every fall. The honest answer: **easiest depends on your skills**, but **official pass rates** and **exam structure** reveal clear patterns.

This ranking uses College Board score distribution trends (see [2026 distributions](/blog/ap-score-distributions)), exam format, and common self-study reports.

## Ranking Criteria

We scored each exam on:

1. **Pass rate (3+)** — higher = more students succeed
2. **Self-study resource availability** — CED quality, AP Daily, past FRQs
3. **Prerequisite difficulty** — math/science background needed?
4. **FRQ complexity** — typed vs handwritten, essay vs short answer
5. **MCQ weight** — higher MCQ % helps self-studiers who drill practice tests

## Tier 1: Best for Self-Study

### 1. AP Psychology
- **Pass rate:** ~62% | **MCQ weight:** ~67%
- **Why:** Conceptual, vocabulary-driven, two straightforward FRQs
- **Prep time:** 8–10 weeks
- **Practice:** [AP Psych practice tests](/exams/ap-psychology) · [Score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-psychology)

### 2. AP Human Geography
- **Pass rate:** ~55% | **Format:** Fully digital typed FRQs
- **Why:** No heavy math; units build logically; strong AP Daily coverage
- **Practice:** [AP HUG exams](/exams/ap-human-geography)

### 3. AP Microeconomics
- **Pass rate:** ~60% | **Format:** Hybrid (3 handwritten FRQs)
- **Why:** Only 6 units; graph patterns repeat; short exam
- **Practice:** [AP Micro exams](/exams/ap-microeconomics)

### 4. AP Computer Science Principles
- **Pass rate:** ~65% | **Format:** Digital + Create performance task
- **Why:** Broad concepts, not deep coding — but Create task needs planning
- **Practice:** [AP CSP exams](/exams/ap-csp)

## Tier 2: Moderate Self-Study

### 5. AP Environmental Science
- Pass rate ~39% but content is readable; FRQs reward memorized examples

### 6. AP Statistics
- Pass rate ~57%; needs Algebra 2; [Stats calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-statistics) helps track progress

### 7. AP Comparative Government
- Smaller course; fully digital; less popular but manageable in 10 weeks

### 8. AP Computer Science A
- Pass rate ~68% IF you already code in Java; otherwise steep climb

## Tier 3: Challenging to Self-Study

| Exam | Why it's harder solo |
|---|---|
| AP Calculus BC | Heavy content; needs strong precalc + calc foundation |
| AP Physics C | Calculus-based; few self-study resources for intuition |
| AP English Literature | FRQ quality needs feedback; literary analysis is skill-based |
| AP Chemistry | Lab-style FRQs; math + memorization combo |
| AP U.S. History | Essay-heavy (60% FRQ); needs DBQ/LEQ practice |

For Calc AB vs BC decision: [AB vs BC guide](/blog/ap-calculus-ab-vs-bc)

## How to Pick YOUR Easiest AP

Ask yourself:

1. **What am I already good at?** — Match exam to strengths
2. **How many hours/week can I commit?** — 5 hrs/week ≠ same plan as 15
3. **Do I have AP Classroom access?** — Through any enrolled school course
4. **What's my goal — credit or rigor?** — A 3 on Psych still counts at many colleges ([details](/blog/is-a-3-on-ap-exam-good))
