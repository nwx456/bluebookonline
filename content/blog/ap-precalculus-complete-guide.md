**Short answer:** The 2026 AP Precalculus exam is Tue, May 12, 2026 (8 AM Local) — 40 MCQs, 180 minutes total, hybrid digital (MCQ in Bluebook, paper FRQs).

The 2026 AP Precalculus exam is **Tuesday, May 12, 2026 (8 AM local)**. Launched in 2023, it bridges algebra II and calculus with polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric modeling. 2025 pass rate was ~81% — one of the highest among AP math exams.

## How the 2026 AP Precalculus Exam Is Structured

Hybrid digital — **3 hours total**.

| Section | Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section I Part A (MCQ, no calculator) | 28 | ~44% |
| Section I Part B (MCQ, calculator) | 12 | ~19% |
| Section II (FRQ, mixed calculator) | 4 | ~37% |

**Total:** 40 MCQs + 4 FRQs (6 points each).

## Unit Weights (Exam = Units 1–3 Only)

| Unit | Topic | Exam Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Polynomial and Rational Functions | 30–40% |
| 2 | Exponential and Logarithmic Functions | 27–40% |
| 3 | Trigonometric and Polar Functions | 30–35% |
| 4 | Parameters, Vectors, Matrices | Not on exam |

Unit 1 (polynomial behavior, asymptotes, inequalities) and Unit 3 (sinusoidal modeling, polar) are the heaviest.

## FRQ Tips

- **Show all work** — partial credit is generous when reasoning is clear.
- **Context matters** — many FRQs are real-world modeling (population, temperature, periodic phenomena).
- **Calculator FRQs** — use regression, intersection, and table features efficiently.

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Unit 1 — end behavior, zeros, rational asymptotes.

**Weeks 3–4:** Unit 2 — log rules, exponential growth/decay, inverse functions.

**Weeks 5–6:** Unit 3 — unit circle, sinusoidal graphs, polar conversions.
