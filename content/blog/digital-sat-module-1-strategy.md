Module 1 is the **gatekeeper** of Digital SAT scoring. Your performance determines whether Module 2 unlocks a top score or caps you on the easier path. This guide gives you a concrete Module 1 playbook for both Reading & Writing and Math.

> **Related:** [Adaptive testing explained](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works) · [R&W guide](/blog/digital-sat-reading-writing-complete-guide) · [Math guide](/blog/digital-sat-math-complete-guide)

## The Module 1 Mindset

**Goal:** Maximize correct answers on questions you are capable of answering — not finish every question.

Module 1 is not where you take risks on hard inference or multi-step word problems. It is where you **bank points** on grammar, basic algebra, and straightforward data questions.

## Reading & Writing Module 1 (27 questions, 32 min)

### Question Order Strategy

1. **First pass (20 min):** Answer every grammar/conventions question and any passage question you can solve in under 60 seconds.
2. **Flag (not skip):** Hard inference, cross-text connections, and synthesis questions.
3. **Second pass (12 min):** Return to flagged questions. Guess on anything still unresolved.

### Accuracy Targets

| Target | Approximate routing |
|---|---|
| 15–17 correct | Likely easy Module 2 |
| 18–20 correct | Borderline — depends on item difficulty |
| 21+ correct | Strong chance of hard Module 2 |

### High-Leverage Module 1 Tactics

- **Read the question before the passage** on inference items — you often need only 2–3 sentences.
- **Eliminate two choices immediately** on vocabulary questions, then compare remaining options.
- **Never spend 3+ minutes on one question** in Module 1 — flag it.

## Math Module 1 (22 questions, 35 min)

### Question Order Strategy

1. **First pass:** All single-step algebra, data analysis, and geometry questions.
2. **Use Desmos immediately** on systems, intersections, and graph-based questions.
3. **Flag** multi-step word problems and unfamiliar advanced math.
4. **Second pass:** Grid-ins and flagged items.

### Accuracy Targets

| Target | Approximate routing |
|---|---|
| 12–14 correct | Likely easy Module 2 |
| 15–17 correct | Borderline |
| 18+ correct | Strong chance of hard Module 2 |

### High-Leverage Module 1 Tactics

- **Plug in (C) or (D)** on multiple-choice algebra — saves setup time.
- **Graph with Desmos** instead of solving systems algebraically. See [Desmos tips](/blog/desmos-sat-math-tips).
- **Enter grid-in best estimates** — never leave blank.

## Common Module 1 Mistakes

1. **Treating Module 1 like a speed drill** — rushing causes careless errors on easy questions.
2. **Not flagging** — students who never use mark-for-review waste time re-reading.
3. **Perfectionism on one hard question** — costs 2–3 easy questions elsewhere.

## Practice Drill

Take **Module 1 only** from Bluebook Practice Test 1 under strict time:

- R&W: 32 minutes, stop at the bell
- Math: 35 minutes, stop at the bell

Score each module, then enter results in the [SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator). Repeat weekly until you consistently hit routing thresholds.

## After Module 1

Once Module 2 begins, the routing decision is locked. Focus shifts to maximizing your score within your assigned path. If you got the hard path, expect harder questions — but also a higher ceiling.
