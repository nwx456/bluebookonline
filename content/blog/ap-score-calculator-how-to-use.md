You finished a practice test. You got 38/45 MCQs right and scored 28/54 FRQ points. **What AP score does that translate to?**

That's what [AP score calculators](/tools/ap-score-calculator) answer — using the same section weights published in each course's [Course and Exam Description (CED)](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses).

## Step 2: Enter MCQ Correct

Count how many multiple-choice questions you answered correctly. **No penalty for wrong answers** on AP exams — so always guess.

Use the stepper or presets (50%, 65%, 75%, 85%, 100%) to quickly test scenarios.

## Step 3: Enter FRQ Points

Enter points earned on each FRQ part. Match the rubric from your practice:

- **History exams:** SAQ (9 pts) + DBQ (7 pts) + LEQ (6 pts)
- **Science exams:** individual FRQ point totals
- **Essays (Lang/Lit):** each essay scored 0–6

## Step 4: Read Your Composite and Predicted Score

The calculator:

1. Weights MCQ and FRQ using **official CED percentages**
2. Combines into a composite score
3. Maps composite to a predicted **1–5** using historical cutoffs

See [how AP exams are scored](/blog/how-ap-exams-are-scored) for the full equating explanation.

## What Calculators Cannot Do

- Predict exact July scores (equating shifts cutoffs annually)
- Replace official College Board scoring
- Account for form difficulty differences

Use them to **guide study priorities** — if FRQs drag your composite below a 3, shift time from MCQ drilling to essay/FRQ practice.
