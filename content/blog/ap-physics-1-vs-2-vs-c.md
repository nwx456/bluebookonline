Four AP Physics exams exist. Picking the wrong one wastes a year. Here's the breakdown.

## Comparison Table

| Exam | Math Level | Content | MCQ/FRQ | Pass Rate (2025) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Physics 1 | Algebra | Mechanics | 40/4 | ~66% |
| Physics 2 | Algebra | E&M, optics, thermo, modern | 40/4 | ~72% |
| Physics C: Mech | Calculus | Mechanics (deeper) | 40/4 | ~72% |
| Physics C: E&M | Calculus | Electricity & magnetism | 40/4 | ~72% |

All four use **hybrid digital** format in 2026 (MCQ in Bluebook, handwritten FRQs).

## AP Physics 1

Best first physics AP. Algebra-based mechanics: kinematics, forces, energy, momentum, rotation, fluids.

[Physics 1 guide](/blog/ap-physics-1-complete-guide) · [Calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-physics-1)

## AP Physics 2

Continues with **non-mechanics** topics. Requires Physics 1 as prerequisite in most schools.

[Physics 2 guide](/blog/ap-physics-2-complete-guide) · [Calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-physics-2)

## AP Physics C

Two separate exams — **Mechanics** and **E&M** — each 90 minutes. Calculus required.

[C Mech guide](/blog/ap-physics-c-mechanics-complete-guide) · [C E&M guide](/blog/ap-physics-c-em-complete-guide)

## Decision Guide

- **9th–10th grade, first physics:** Physics 1
- **After Physics 1, broad science interest:** Physics 2
- **Strong calculus, engineering/physics major:** Physics C Mechanics (± E&M same year)
- **Never take Physics 1 and C Mechanics for the same credit** — they overlap; pick one path
