Self-studying an AP exam — without a teacher, class period, or grade pressure — is absolutely doable. Students on r/APStudents regularly report 4s and 5s on AP Psychology, Human Geography, Microeconomics, and Computer Science A through solo prep.

This guide gives you a repeatable system: **CED → resources → practice → feedback loop**, using free official materials and [AP Practice Exam Online](/exams) tools.

## Step 1: Choose a Self-Study-Friendly AP

Not every AP is equal for solo learners. See our ranked list: [Easiest AP exams to self-study](/blog/easiest-ap-exams-to-self-study).

**Strong self-study candidates:**
- AP Psychology, Human Geography, Microeconomics
- AP Computer Science A (with prior coding)
- AP Environmental Science (content-heavy but approachable)
- AP Statistics (with Algebra 2 background)

**Harder to self-study:**
- AP Physics C (calculus-based)
- AP English Literature (discussion and feedback help)
- AP Art portfolio exams (teacher mentorship valuable)

Use [pass rate data](/blog/ap-score-distributions) and honest skill assessment before committing.

## Step 2: Download the Course and Exam Description (CED)

The **CED** is your syllabus — free on [AP Central](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses):

1. Find your course page
2. Download the CED PDF
3. Note **unit weights** (% of exam per unit)
4. Read the **exam format** section (MCQ count, FRQ types, calculator policy)
5. Check if your exam is [fully digital or hybrid](/blog/fully-digital-vs-hybrid-ap-exams)

**Study time allocation:** Spend proportional time on high-weight units. For AP Bio, Units 6–7 (gene expression, natural selection) outweigh Unit 1.

## Step 3: Gather Official Free Resources

Don't pay for prep until you've exhausted free official materials: [Free official AP study resources guide](/blog/free-official-ap-study-resources).

Essentials:
- **AP Daily videos** (short concept reviews)
- **AP Classroom** progress checks and practice exams (ask a teacher for access, or use school enrollment)
- **Past FRQs** with scoring guidelines on AP Central
- **Bluebook practice exams** (install the app early)

## Step 4: Build a Weekly Schedule

| Week phase | Focus | Hours/week |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Content learning (CED units + AP Daily) | 6–8 |
| Weeks 5–8 | Unit quizzes + MCQ drills | 8–10 |
| Weeks 9–12 | Full timed practice + FRQ writing | 10–12 |
| Final 2 weeks | Review mistakes + light cram | 6–8 |

Cramming? See [1-month AP cram plan](/blog/one-month-ap-study-plan).

## Step 5: Practice Under Real Conditions

Self-studiers often fail by reading passively. You need **timed, scored practice**:

1. **MCQ:** Take [Bluebook-style practice tests](/exams) for your subject
2. **FRQ:** Fully digital exams — practice typing on [FRQ module](/dashboard/upload?kind=frq); hybrid exams — handwritten past FRQs
3. **Score yourself:** Use [AP score calculators](/tools/ap-score-calculator) to track composite trends
4. **Review mistakes:** Log errors in [Mistake Review](/dashboard/mistakes)

## Step 6: Create Custom Practice From Your Notes

When you finish a unit, upload your notes to [Generate Exam](/dashboard/generate):

- Select subject and difficulty
- Choose 10–20 questions
- Take the quiz immediately while content is fresh

This closes the gap between "I read the chapter" and "I can answer AP-level questions."

## Step 7: Register for the Exam

Self-studiers must register through a **school's AP coordinator** or find a participating test center. Deadline is typically **November** for May exams — check [College Board registration](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/register-for-ap-exams).

## Common Self-Study Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Only reading textbooks | Add timed MCQ every week |
| Ignoring FRQs | FRQs are 33–60% of most exams |
| No score tracking | Use calculators + [Insights](/dashboard/insights) |
| Wrong exam format prep | Check digital vs hybrid for YOUR exam |
| Cramming content, not skills | Practice application questions, not flashcards alone |

## Track Progress to a 5

1. Baseline: one untimed practice set
2. Week 4: first timed half-exam
3. Week 8: full timed exam — estimate score
4. Week 12: second full exam — target improvement in weakest section

Read [how AP scoring works](/blog/how-ap-exams-are-scored) so you know which section to prioritize.
