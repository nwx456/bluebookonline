AP Environmental Science FRQs are **40% of your score** — three questions, 10 points each, typed in Bluebook. Official rubrics: [AP Central APES scoring guidelines](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-environmental-science/exam/past-exam-questions).

## The Three FRQ Types

### FRQ 1: Design an Investigation (10 pts)

**Task:** Propose a valid scientific investigation.

**Earn points by including:**
- Clear hypothesis (if/then format)
- Independent and dependent variables identified
- Control group described
- Specific procedure (step-by-step, replicable)
- Data collection method
- How to analyze/graph results

**Example prompt:** "Design an experiment to test whether fertilizer type affects plant growth rate."

### FRQ 2: Analyze an Environmental Problem (10 pts)

**Task:** Explain an environmental issue using course concepts.

**Earn points by:**
- Identifying the environmental problem clearly
- Explaining cause-and-effect relationships
- Proposing a realistic solution with tradeoffs
- Using specific vocabulary (biodiversity, carrying capacity, pollution types)

**Tip:** Always address **both** environmental AND human dimensions.

### FRQ 3: Analyze with Calculation (10 pts)

**Task:** Perform calculations and interpret results in context.

**Earn points by:**
- Setting up the calculation correctly (show work)
- Using correct units throughout
- Interpreting the result in environmental context
- Explaining what the number means (not just computing it)

**Common calculations:** percent change, population growth, energy efficiency, water quality indices.

## Universal FRQ Rules

1. **Answer every part** — even partial responses earn points
2. **Use environmental vocabulary** from the CED
3. **Show units** on all calculations
4. **Be specific** — "deforestation in the Amazon" beats "environmental damage"
