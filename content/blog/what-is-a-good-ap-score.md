**Short answer:** A **good AP score** is usually **3+** (passing), **4** (well qualified), or **5** (extremely well qualified) — but “good” depends on your target college’s credit policy and how competitive your intended major is.

## AP Score Scale (1–5)

| Score | College Board label | Typical meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Extremely well qualified | Strong credit at most schools; top tier for admissions context |
| 4 | Well qualified | Credit at many schools; solid admissions signal |
| 3 | Qualified | Passing; credit at some state schools |
| 2 | Possibly qualified | Rarely earns credit |
| 1 | No recommendation | Does not demonstrate qualification |

## What Counts as “Good” by Goal

### For college credit
Check each university’s [AP credit policy](/blog/ap-credit-policy-explained). Many flagship state schools grant credit for 3s in humanities but require 4s or 5s for Calculus, Chemistry, or Physics.

### For admissions
Selective colleges expect you to take the hardest APs available and perform reasonably well. A 5 in AP Calc BC plus a 3 in APUSH is a common pattern — context matters.

### For your own confidence
If you scored at or above your practice-test estimates using our [AP score calculators](/tools/ap-score-calculator), you likely performed as expected.

## Subject Matters

A 3 on AP Chemistry is harder-earned than a 3 on some introductory courses. Compare pass rates: [2026 AP score distributions](/blog/ap-score-distributions).
