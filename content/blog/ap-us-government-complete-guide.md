**Short answer:** The 2026 AP United States Government and Politics exam is Tue, May 5, 2026 (12 PM Local) — 55 MCQs, 180 minutes total, fully digital in Bluebook.

The 2026 AP U.S. Government exam is **Tuesday, May 5, 2026 (12 PM local)**.

## Exam Structure

Fully digital — **3 hours**.

| Section | Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section I (MCQ) | 55 | 50% |
| Section II (FRQ) | 4 | 50% |

### Four FRQs

| FRQ | Task | Points |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Concept Application | 3 |
| 2 | Quantitative Analysis | 4 |
| 3 | SCOTUS Comparison | 4 |
| 4 | Argument Essay | 6 |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Foundations of American Democracy | 15–22% |
| 2 | Interactions Among Branches | 25–36% |
| 3 | Civil Liberties and Civil Rights | 13–18% |
| 4 | Political Ideologies and Beliefs | 10–15% |
| 5 | Political Participation | 20–27% |

**Units 2 + 5 = ~45–63%** — Congress, presidency, courts, elections, and media.

## Must-Know Content

- **9 foundational documents** (Declaration, Constitution, Federalist 10/51, etc.)
- **15 required Supreme Court cases** — see [SCOTUS cases guide](/blog/ap-us-government-scotus-cases)
- **Data literacy** — read graphs, tables, and polling data for FRQ 2

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Units 1–2 (Constitution + branches).

**Weeks 3–4:** Units 3–5 (rights + participation). Drill SCOTUS cases daily.

**Weeks 5–6:** Released FRQs — especially SCOTUS comparison and argument essay.
