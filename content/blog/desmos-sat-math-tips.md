Every Digital SAT Math question includes a **built-in Desmos graphing calculator**. Students who ignore it leave minutes on the table — minutes that matter on a 35-minute module.

This guide covers the highest-value Desmos techniques for SAT Math, with examples mapped to common question types.

> **Related:** [Math complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-math-complete-guide) · [Grid-in guide](/blog/sat-grid-in-student-produced-response-guide) · [Bluebook interface](/blog/bluebook-sat-interface-explained)

## Technique 1: Graph Systems of Equations

**When to use:** "What is the value of x?" or "What is the solution (x, y) to the system?"

**How:**
1. Type the first equation (e.g., `y = 2x + 3`)
2. Type the second equation (e.g., `y = -x + 6`)
3. Click the intersection point — Desmos shows `(x, y)`

**Time saved:** 60–90 seconds vs algebraic substitution.

## Technique 2: Find Zeros and Vertex

**When to use:** Quadratic questions asking for roots, maximum, or minimum.

**How:**
1. Enter the quadratic: `y = x^2 - 4x + 3`
2. Click x-intercepts for roots
3. Click the vertex for max/min value

**Tip:** If the question gives `(x - h)^2 + k` form, the vertex is at `(h, k)`.

## Technique 3: Inequalities and Shading

**When to use:** "Which region represents the solution set?"

**How:**
1. Enter the inequality: `y > 2x - 1`
2. Desmos shades the solution region
3. Compare with answer choices

## Technique 4: Regression for Scatterplots

**When to use:** Data questions with a table of `(x, y)` values asking for a line of best fit or predicted value.

**How:**
1. Click the `+` button → **table**
2. Enter x-values in column 1, y-values in column 2
3. Desmos auto-fits a regression line
4. Read slope, intercept, or plug in a value

## Technique 5: Plug In With Slider (Advanced)

For "what value of k makes the system have no solution?" type questions:

1. Graph both equations with a parameter: `y = kx + 2` and `y = 3x - 1`
2. Add a slider for `k`
3. Adjust until lines are parallel (no intersection)

## When NOT to Use Desmos

- **Simple arithmetic** — typing `15% of 240` is slower than mental math
- **Pure algebra manipulation** — factoring `(x+3)(x-2) = 0` is faster by hand
- **Conceptual questions** — "which expression represents the average rate of change?" may not need graphing

## Practice Plan

1. Open Bluebook Practice Test 1 Math section.
2. For every question, ask: **"Could Desmos help here?"**
3. Time yourself with and without Desmos on the same question types.
