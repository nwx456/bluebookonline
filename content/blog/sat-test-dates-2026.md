Planning your **2026 SAT test dates** early prevents the most common mistake: registering too late for a retake before college application deadlines.

This guide lists confirmed 2026 dates, registration timelines, and a planning framework for juniors and seniors.

## 2026 SAT Test Dates (U.S.)

| Test Date | Recommended Registration | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **March 8, 2026** | By early February | Ideal first sitting for juniors |
| **May 3, 2026** | By early April | Strong retake window; before AP exams peak |
| **June 7, 2026** | By early May | Last spring date; good for final junior retake |
| **August 23, 2026** | By late July | First fall date; popular for summer prep |
| **October 4, 2026** | By early September | Critical for early action deadlines |
| **November 8, 2026** | By early October | Last chance before many ED/EA deadlines |
| **December 6, 2026** | By early November | Final 2026 date; scores arrive before January |

*Verify exact registration deadlines at [satsuite.collegeboard.org](https://satsuite.collegeboard.org). Dates subject to College Board confirmation.*

## Score Release Timeline

Scores typically release **13–15 days** after the test date. Plan backward from college deadlines:

| Application Deadline | Latest SAT Sitting |
|---|---|
| Early Decision (Nov 1) | October (maybe August retake) |
| Early Action (Nov 15) | October |
| Regular Decision (Jan 1) | November or December |
| Regular Decision (Jan 15+) | December |

**Superscore note:** If your target colleges superscore, you need all sittings to complete before deadlines. See [superscore guide](/blog/sat-superscore-explained).

| Sitting | Purpose |

### Seniors (Class of 2026)

| Sitting | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **August 2026** | Final retake before EA/ED |
| **October 2026** | Last chance for November deadlines |
| **November–December 2026** | Only if RD deadlines allow |

## Registration Tips

1. **Register early** — popular test centers fill up, especially August and October.
2. **Use your legal name** — must match photo ID exactly.
3. **Choose a familiar location** — reduce test-day stress with a known building.
4. **Send scores later** — you can send to 4 colleges free within 9 days of scores releasing.
