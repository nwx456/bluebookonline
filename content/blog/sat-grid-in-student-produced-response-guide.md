**Student-produced response** questions — commonly called **grid-ins** — make up about **25% of Digital SAT Math**. They have no answer choices, which makes them the highest-anxiety question type for many students. This guide covers entry rules, strategy, and the mistakes that cost points.

## Grid-In Format Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Character limit | 5 characters max |
| Negative answers | Not accepted — recheck if negative |
| Fractions | Enter as `3/4` (counts as 3 characters) |
| Decimals | Enter as `0.75` or `.75` |
| Mixed numbers | Convert to improper fraction or decimal — do not enter `2 1/2` |
| Leading zeros | Not required — `.5` equals `0.5` |
| Equivalent forms | `2`, `2.0`, and `2/1` may all be accepted |

## Where Grid-Ins Appear

Grid-ins are **mixed into both Math modules** — not clustered at the end. Expect roughly 5–6 per module. They appear alongside multiple-choice questions in random order.

## Strategy: Treat Grid-Ins Like Multiple-Choice

1. **Solve the problem fully** — do not guess from answer choice patterns (there are none).
2. **Double-check units** — if the question asks for "hours" and you have minutes, convert.
3. **Verify reasonableness** — a probability grid-in should be between 0 and 1; an area should be positive.
4. **Enter and move on** — do not second-guess format if your answer fits the rules.

## Common Grid-In Mistakes

### 1. Rounding Too Early
If the exact answer is `1/3`, enter `1/3` — not `0.33`. Rounded decimals may not match the accepted answer.

### 2. Exceeding 5 Characters
`10/12` works (5 chars). `100/12` does not (6 chars) — simplify to `25/3` or enter the decimal.

### 3. Forgetting to Convert
A question asks for the answer "in terms of π" but the grid expects a decimal? Read the prompt carefully — most grid-ins want a numeric value, not an expression with π.

### 4. Leaving Blank
There is **no penalty for wrong grid-in answers**. Always enter your best estimate.

## Pacing Grid-Ins

Grid-ins typically take **30–60 seconds longer** than equivalent multiple-choice questions because there is no elimination shortcut.

**Module 1 tactic:** Flag grid-ins that require multi-step setup. Knock out quick grid-ins (single-step algebra) first.

**Module 2 tactic:** If running low on time, enter reasonable estimates for remaining grid-ins rather than leaving blank.
