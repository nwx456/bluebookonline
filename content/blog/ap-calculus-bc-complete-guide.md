The 2026 AP Calculus BC exam is **Monday, May 11, 2026 (8 AM local)**. BC covers all AB content plus parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, and infinite series. Pass rates are typically higher than AB (~79% earn a 3+ in 2025), but earning a 5 requires mastery of series convergence tests and polar area/volume.

## How the 2026 AP Calculus BC Exam Is Structured

Hybrid digital exam — **3 hours 15 minutes total**. Section I in Bluebook; Section II prompts on screen, answers handwritten in a paper booklet.

| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Section I Part A (MCQ, no calculator) | 30 | 60 min | ~33% |
| Section I Part B (MCQ, calculator) | 15 | 45 min | ~17% |
| Section II Part A (FRQ, calculator) | 2 | 30 min | ~17% |
| Section II Part B (FRQ, no calculator) | 4 | 60 min | ~33% |

**Total MCQ:** 45 questions (50% of score). **Total FRQ:** 6 questions (50% of score).

## Unit-by-Unit Weights (CED)

| Unit | Topic | Exam Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Limits and Continuity | 4–7% |
| 2 | Differentiation: Definition | 4–7% |
| 3 | Composite, Implicit, Inverse | 4–7% |
| 4 | Contextual Applications | 6–9% |
| 5 | Analytical Applications | 8–11% |
| 6 | Integration and Accumulation | 17–20% |
| 7 | Differential Equations | 6–9% |
| 8 | Applications of Integration | 6–9% |
| 9 | Parametric, Polar, Vectors | 11–12% |
| 10 | Infinite Sequences and Series | 17–18% |

**Units 6, 9, and 10 are the BC differentiators.** Series (Unit 10) appears on almost every FRQ section.

## FRQ Archetypes

1. **Calculator-active problems** — definite integrals, differential equations, Euler's method.
2. **Non-calculator problems** — limits, derivatives, series convergence, Taylor/Maclaurin polynomials.
3. **Series-specific** — ratio test, alternating series error bound, power series radius of convergence.
4. **Parametric/polar** — arc length, area enclosed by polar curves, vector motion.
