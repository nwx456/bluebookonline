*"Is my SAT score good?"* is one of the highest-volume search queries every testing season. The honest answer: **it depends on your target colleges** — but national percentiles give you a useful baseline.

This guide breaks down what **1200**, **1300**, and **1400** mean in 2026, with percentile context and retake guidance.

|---|---|---|
| 1200 | ~75th | Above average |
| 1300 | ~87th | Strong |
| 1400 | ~95th | Excellent |

Percentiles shift slightly each year. See our full [percentile breakdown](/blog/sat-score-percentiles-2026) for updated data.

## Is 1200 a Good SAT Score?

**National context:** 1200 (600 R&W + 600 Math) places you around the **75th percentile** — you scored higher than about three-quarters of test takers.

**College context:**
- **Competitive for:** Many state universities, regional colleges, test-optional schools that still consider scores
- **Below median at:** Most top-50 national universities and selective liberal arts colleges
- **Merit aid:** May qualify at some schools; unlikely at the most selective

**Should you retake?** If your target schools have a middle 50% starting at 1250+, a focused retake is worthwhile. See [improve 100 points](/blog/how-to-improve-sat-score-100-points).

## Is 1300 a Good SAT Score?

**National context:** 1300 is approximately the **87th percentile** — a genuinely strong score.

**College context:**
- **Competitive for:** Many top-50 universities, strong liberal arts colleges, honors programs at state flagships
- **Below median at:** Ivy League, Stanford, MIT, and similarly selective schools (middle 50% often 1500+)
- **Merit aid:** Strong candidate at many schools

**Should you retake?** If your dream school’s middle 50% is 1400–1500, retaking your weaker section with [superscore strategy](/blog/sat-superscore-explained) can help. If 1300 exceeds your targets, move on to essays and activities.

## Is 1400 a Good SAT Score?

**National context:** 1400 is roughly the **95th percentile** — top 5% nationally.

**College context:**
- **Competitive for:** Most selective universities outside the very top tier
- **Below median at:** Harvard, Yale, Princeton, Stanford (middle 50% often 1500–1570)
- **Merit aid:** Excellent position at most schools

**Should you retake?** Only if specific target schools have medians above 1450+ and you have a clear weakness to fix. Diminishing returns set in above 1400 for most students.
