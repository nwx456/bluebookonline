Junior year dilemma: **AP English Language** or wait for **AP English Literature**? Or both?

## Quick Comparison

| | AP Lang | AP Lit |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Rhetoric, argument, nonfiction | Literary analysis: poetry, prose, drama |
| MCQs | 45 (45%) | 55 (45%) |
| Essays | Synthesis, Rhetorical Analysis, Argument | Poetry, Prose Fiction, Literary Argument |
| Essay weight | 55% | 55% |
| Format | Fully digital | Fully digital |

## AP English Language

You analyze **how authors persuade** — speeches, essays, political writing. Three essay types:

1. **Synthesis** — combine sources into an argument
2. **Rhetorical Analysis** — analyze how a text works
3. **Argument** — defend a position

[Lang complete guide](/blog/ap-english-language-complete-guide) · [Calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-english-language)

## AP English Literature

You analyze **literary works** — poetry, fiction, drama. Three essay types:

1. **Poetry Analysis**
2. **Prose Fiction Analysis**
3. **Literary Argument** (about a work you choose)

[Lit complete guide](/blog/ap-english-literature-complete-guide) · [Calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-english-literature)

## Which Fits You?

**Choose Lang if:** You enjoy debate, journalism, politics, or analyzing arguments.

**Choose Lit if:** You love reading novels/poetry and close textual analysis.

**Strong writers** can succeed in either — the essay rubrics are similar (thesis + evidence + sophistication).
