**Short answer:** Digital SAT Reading & Writing is 64 minutes — two adaptive modules of 27 questions each, no calculator, scored 200–800.

Digital SAT **Reading & Writing** (R&W) is one half of your total 400–1600 score, scaled from 200 to 800. Unlike the old SAT's long reading passages, the digital format uses **short texts with one question each** — which changes pacing and strategy significantly.

This guide covers the four College Board skill domains, timing, Module 1 tactics, and the highest-leverage prep moves for 2026.

## Section Format

| Module | Questions | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Module 1 | 27 | 32 min |
| Module 2 | 27 | 32 min |

Module 2 difficulty adapts based on Module 1. See [how adaptive testing works](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works).

## Four Skill Domains

College Board organizes R&W into four domains (approximate weights):

### 1. Craft and Structure (~28%)
- Words in context (vocabulary)
- Text structure and purpose
- Cross-text connections (comparing two short passages)

**Tip:** For vocabulary questions, plug each answer choice back into the sentence — the correct word must fit both meaning and tone.

### 2. Information and Ideas (~26%)
- Central ideas and details
- Command of evidence (textual and quantitative)
- Inferences

**Tip:** Quantitative questions often include a simple chart or table. Read the question first, then locate the relevant data — do not read the entire passage first.

### 3. Standard English Conventions (~26%)
- Boundaries (commas, semicolons, dashes)
- Form, structure, and sense (subject-verb agreement, pronouns, verb tense)

**Tip:** Read the sentence aloud in your head. Your ear catches boundary errors faster than your eyes.

### 4. Expression of Ideas (~20%)
- Rhetorical synthesis (combining notes into an effective sentence)
- Transitions

**Tip:** On synthesis questions, identify the **goal stated in the prompt** before evaluating answer choices.

## Pacing: The 70-Second Rule

Average time: **~71 seconds per question**. Practical pacing:

- **Grammar/conventions (Questions 1–15 feel):** 45–55 seconds each
- **Passage inference:** 75–90 seconds each
- **Mark and return:** Flag anything over 90 seconds; do not burn time early

Use the question grid in Bluebook to jump to flagged items in the final 5 minutes. See the [Bluebook SAT interface guide](/blog/bluebook-sat-interface-explained).

## Module 1 Strategy

Module 1 determines your Module 2 path. For R&W:

1. **Accuracy over speed in Module 1.** Missing easy grammar questions hurts more than running out of time on one hard inference.
2. **Do not leave blanks.** No penalty for guessing.
3. **Target 18+ correct in Module 1** as a rough threshold for the hard Module 2 path (varies by form).

Full playbook: [Digital SAT Module 1 strategy](/blog/digital-sat-module-1-strategy).

## Common Mistakes

- **Over-reading short passages.** One question per passage means you often need only 2–3 sentences.
- **Ignoring the "student notes" format** on synthesis questions — the prompt tells you exactly what the answer must accomplish.
- **Spending equal time on every question.** Grammar should be faster; budget time for inference.
