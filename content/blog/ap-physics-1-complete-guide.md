The 2026 AP Physics 1 exam is **Wednesday, May 6, 2026 (12 PM local)**. Algebra-based mechanics covering kinematics through fluids. Pass rates improved significantly in 2025 (~66% earned 3+) after the exam redesign.

## Exam Structure (2025–26 CED)

Hybrid digital — **3 hours**. Calculator permitted on both sections.

| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Section I (MCQ) | 40 | 80 min | 50% |
| Section II (FRQ) | 4 | 100 min | 50% |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Kinematics | 10–15% |
| 2 | Force and Translational Dynamics | 18–23% |
| 3 | Work, Energy, and Power | 18–23% |
| 4 | Linear Momentum | 10–15% |
| 5 | Torque and Rotational Dynamics | 10–15% |
| 6 | Energy/Momentum of Rotating Systems | 5–8% |
| 7 | Oscillations | 5–8% |
| 8 | Fluids | 10–15% |

**Units 2 + 3 = ~36–46%** of your score. Master free-body diagrams and energy bar charts first.

## FRQ Archetypes

1. **Experimental design** — propose a procedure, identify variables, justify equipment.
2. **Qualitative/quantitative translation** — explain then calculate.
3. **Paragraph-length response** — defend a claim with physics principles.

Always **show units**, **draw diagrams**, and **cite fundamental principles** (Newton's laws, conservation laws).

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Units 1–2 (kinematics + forces). 20 MCQ/day.

**Weeks 3–4:** Units 3–4 (energy + momentum). Practice energy bar charts.

**Weeks 5–6:** Units 5–8 (rotation, oscillations, fluids).

**Week 7:** Released FRQs + [Physics 1 score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-physics-1).
