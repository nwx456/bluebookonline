**2026 AP scores begin releasing Monday, July 6, 2026.** That's the date College Board confirmed for this year's score rollout — and every year, millions of students refresh their College Board account hoping their 1–5 appears first.

Here's the complete timeline, how to access scores, what to do if they're delayed, and how to estimate your results before July using practice data.

## 2026 AP Score Release Timeline

| Milestone | Date |
|---|---|
| Last regular AP exams | Mid-May 2026 |
| Late-testing window | Late May 2026 |
| FRQ scoring completed | June 2026 |
| **Score release begins** | **Monday, July 6, 2026** |
| Score sending to colleges | July–August (if requested) |

Scores release **by geographic region on a rolling schedule** — not all states at the exact same minute. East Coast states often appear first; West Coast may follow hours later.

## How to Check Your AP Scores

1. Go to [apstudents.collegeboard.org](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org)
2. Log in with the **same account** you used to register for exams
3. Navigate to **View Your Scores**
4. Scores appear as 1–5 for each exam taken

**Important:** If you created multiple College Board accounts, scores may be on the account linked to your AP registration — not your SAT account.

## What If Your Scores Aren't There on July 6?

Common reasons for delays:

| Cause | Solution |
|---|---|
| Late-testing exam | Scores release 1–2 weeks after regular batch |
| Wrong College Board account | Log into the account used at registration |
| Scoring hold (irregularity report) | Contact AP Services for Students |
| Missing exam entirely | Verify with your AP coordinator |

Contact **AP Services for Students** if scores haven't appeared by **August 15**.

## Predict Your Score Before July

While you wait, use practice data to estimate:

1. Take a full-length [practice exam](/exams) under timed conditions
2. Enter MCQ correct + FRQ points into our [AP score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator)
3. Read [how AP scoring works](/blog/how-ap-exams-are-scored) to understand equating

Remember: calculators provide **estimates**. Official scores include professional FRQ grading and annual equating adjustments.

## What to Do After Scores Release

### If you scored 4 or 5
- Send scores to colleges via College Board (free sends may be limited — check current policy)
- Search [AP credit policies](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/getting-credit-placement/search-policies) for your target schools
- Celebrate — then optionally help classmates prep next year

### If you scored 3
- Read [Is a 3 on an AP Exam Good?](/blog/is-a-3-on-ap-exam-good) for credit and admissions context
- Check if your target colleges accept 3s

### If you scored 1 or 2
- Request your school's AP coordinator for **free-response booklets** (hybrid exams) if you want to review
- Analyze weak sections using [Mistake Review](/dashboard/mistakes) on practice platforms
- Plan retake strategy if your school allows and the subject matters for your goals

## Sending Scores to Colleges

- **Free score send:** Often available before a deadline in June (check College Board for current year)
- **Additional sends:** Fee per report after deadline
- **Score withholding:** Available if you don't want a score sent (fee applies)
