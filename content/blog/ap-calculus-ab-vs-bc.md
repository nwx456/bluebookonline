The AB vs BC decision comes up every registration season. Both are **hybrid digital exams** in 2026 (MCQ in Bluebook, handwritten FRQs), both last 3 hours 15 minutes, and **you cannot take both in the same year**.

Here's a clear comparison to help you choose — plus free [Calc AB](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-calculus-ab) and [Calc BC](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-calculus-bc) score calculators.

## Content Comparison

| Topic | AB | BC |
|---|---|---|
| Limits & continuity | ✓ | ✓ |
| Derivatives & applications | ✓ | ✓ |
| Integrals & applications | ✓ | ✓ |
| Differential equations | Basic | Advanced |
| Parametric, polar, vector | ✗ | ✓ |
| Infinite series | ✗ | ✓ (Taylor, convergence tests) |
| **College equivalent** | ~1 semester | ~2 semesters |

BC = **all of AB** plus ~2 additional units of advanced calculus.

## Pass Rates & Score Distributions

From [2026 AP score distributions](/blog/ap-score-distributions):

| Exam | Pass rate (3+) | % earning 5 |
|---|---|---|
| AP Calculus AB | ~58% | ~21% |
| AP Calculus BC | ~81% | ~44% |

BC's higher pass rate reflects **self-selection** — schools often require AB mastery or strong precalc before BC. AB includes more underprepared students.

Don't assume BC is "easier" — the content is strictly more advanced.

## Exam Format (2026)

Both exams share identical structure:

| Section | Details | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| MCQ Part A | 30 questions, no calculator, 60 min | 33.3% |
| MCQ Part B | 15 questions, graphing calculator, 45 min | 16.7% |
| FRQ Part A | 2 questions, calculator, 30 min | 16.7% |
| FRQ Part B | 4 questions, no calculator, 60 min | 33.3% |

**Hybrid format:** MCQ in Bluebook; FRQs viewed on screen, **written in paper booklets**. See [hybrid booklet guide](/blog/hybrid-ap-exam-frq-booklets).

**Calculator policy:** Graphing calculator required on designated sections only — know which parts allow it before exam day.

## Who Should Take AB?

Choose **AP Calculus AB** if:

- This is your **first calculus course**
- Your precalculus foundation has gaps
- Your school places most juniors in AB
- You're a senior taking one math AP for credit
- STEM isn't your primary direction

**Prep:** [Calc AB complete guide](/blog/ap-calculus-ab-complete-guide) · [Practice tests](/exams/ap-calculus-ab) · [Score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-calculus-ab)

## Who Should Take BC?

Choose **AP Calculus BC** if:

- You completed AB already (or equivalent content)
- You're strong in precalculus and enjoy fast-paced math
- You're targeting **engineering, physics, or math** majors
- Your school sequences AB junior year → BC senior year

**Prep:** [Practice tests](/exams/ap-calculus-bc) · [Score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator/ap-calculus-bc)

## College Credit Implications

Credit policies vary, but common patterns:

| Score | AB typical credit | BC typical credit |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 1 semester calc | 2 semesters calc |
| 4 | 1 semester (many schools) | 1–2 semesters |
| 3 | Sometimes placement only | 1 semester (some schools) |

Search your target colleges: [AP Credit Policy Search](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/getting-credit-placement/search-policies)

A 3 on either exam may qualify — [what a 3 means](/blog/is-a-3-on-ap-exam-good).

## The AB Exam Subscore on BC

If you take BC, your score report includes an **AB subscore** (1–5) based on AB-only content. If BC goes poorly, a strong AB subscore can still demonstrate AB-level mastery to colleges.

## Can You Skip AB and Go Straight to BC?

Depends on your school. Mathematically possible if you self-study AB content over summer, but **risky** — BC moves fast and assumes AB fluency.

If self-studying, follow [self-study guide](/blog/how-to-self-study-ap-exams) and verify you can score 4+ on AB-level practice before committing to BC.

## Study Strategy for Either Exam

1. **Master calculator vs non-calculator sections separately** — half the exam prohibits calculators
2. **FRQs are 50% of score** — show all work in booklets; practice handwritten FRQs
3. **Use score calculator weekly** — track composite, not just MCQ %
4. **Understand scoring** — [how AP exams are scored](/blog/how-ap-exams-are-scored)

## Quick Decision Chart

```
Strong precalc + STEM major + school offers BC?
  → YES → Take BC
  → NO → Take AB

Already completed AB with A/A-?
  → Take BC senior year

Senior, one math AP needed for credit?
  → AB is sufficient for most non-STEM majors
```
