Exam day anxiety often comes from uncertainty — not the content. Use this **2026 AP exam day checklist** to pack the right items, configure your device, and know what to expect for both **fully digital** and **hybrid digital** Bluebook exams.

## One Week Before: Device & App Prep

- [ ] **Install Bluebook** from [bluebook.app.collegeboard.org](https://bluebook.app.collegeboard.org)
- [ ] **Run the exam setup check** on your testing device
- [ ] **Confirm device meets specs** (Windows, Mac, iPad, or school Chromebook per policy)
- [ ] **Charge fully** the night before; bring charger if allowed
- [ ] **Complete any AP Digital Portfolio** submissions (CSP, Seminar, Art, etc.)
- [ ] **Take a timed practice exam** on [AP Practice Exam Online](/exams) to rehearse pacing

## Night Before

- [ ] Set two alarms
- [ ] Prepare approved ID (government-issued photo ID or school ID per policy)
- [ ] Print or save **AP admission ticket** if your school requires it
- [ ] Pack approved calculator (if your exam allows — Calc, Physics, Stats, etc.)
- [ ] **No last-minute cram past 10 PM** — sleep beats one more chapter
- [ ] Review [fully digital vs hybrid format](/blog/fully-digital-vs-hybrid-ap-exams) for YOUR subjects

## Morning Of: What to Bring

### All digital AP exams
| Item | Required? |
|---|---|
| Testing device with Bluebook | **Yes** |
| Photo ID | **Yes** |
| Admission ticket | Usually |
| Approved calculator | If exam allows |
| Pen/pencil | For hybrid FRQ booklets |
| Snacks/water | For breaks (between exams) |

### Hybrid digital exams (Calc, Bio, Chem, Physics, Stats, Econ)
- [ ] **Pens and/or pencils** — College Board permits either for most hybrid FRQs
- [ ] Know that **proctors distribute paper FRQ booklets** — you don't bring your own
- [ ] Review [hybrid booklet format](/blog/hybrid-ap-exam-frq-booklets) for your subject

### Fully digital exams (APUSH, Lang, Psych, CSA, etc.)
- [ ] **No paper needed** for FRQs — all typed in Bluebook
- [ ] Practice [typed FRQ strategies](/blog/digital-frq-typing-strategies) beforehand

## What NOT to Bring

- Smartphones (must be off and stored — not on your person)
- Smartwatches or fitness trackers
- Notes, textbooks, formula sheets (unless provided in Bluebook)
- Earbuds (unless approved accommodation)
- Another person expecting to wait in the testing room

## During the Exam: Bluebook Procedures

1. **Check in** with proctor; verify device connects to Test Day Toolkit
2. **Enter start code** when proctor announces
3. **Use tools:** highlight, mark-for-review, option eliminate (see [interface guide](/blog/bluebook-interface-explained))
4. **Hybrid only:** At Section II, proctor distributes FRQ booklets — write all work in the booklet
5. **Submit** when timer ends — fully digital exams auto-submit

## After the Exam

- [ ] Don't discuss specific questions online (College Board policy)
- [ ] **Estimate your score** with our [AP score calculator](/tools/ap-score-calculator)
- [ ] Log weak topics in [Mistake Review](/dashboard/mistakes) while memory is fresh
- [ ] Mark calendar: **scores release July 6, 2026** ([details](/blog/when-do-ap-scores-come-out))

|---|---|
| AP Calculus AB/BC | Graphing calculator on designated sections only |
