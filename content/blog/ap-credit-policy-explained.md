A score of 4 on AP Calculus BC might earn **8 credit hours** at one university and **nothing** at another. AP credit is **not universal** — each college sets its own policy.

## How to Look Up Any College's Policy

1. Go to [AP Credit Policy Search](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/getting-credit-placement/search-policies)
2. Search by college name
3. See which AP exams are accepted and minimum scores required

This is the **only official source** for credit policies. Third-party lists go stale.

## Typical Score Thresholds

| AP Score | What It Usually Means |
|---|---|
| 5 | Credit + advanced placement at most selective schools |
| 4 | Credit at many state universities; placement at selective schools |
| 3 | Credit at many public universities; rarely at top-20 schools |
| 2 | Generally no credit |
| 1 | No credit |

See [Is a 3 on an AP exam good?](/blog/is-a-3-on-ap-exam-good) for context.

## Credit vs Placement vs Both

- **Credit only:** You earn hours toward graduation (e.g., "4 credits for AP Bio with score 4+")
- **Placement only:** Skip Intro Biology but take no credit hours
- **Both:** Most common at large public universities

Some schools cap total AP credits (often 24–32 hours).

## Subject-Specific Notes

- **Calculus BC** often earns more credit than AB (BC frequently covers Calc I + II)
- **Language exams** may require an additional departmental exam for placement
- **History/Gov** credits often apply to general education, not major requirements
- **CS exams** policies vary widely — some grant CS major credit, others only elective

## Plan Before You Take the Exam

1. List your target colleges in the [Credit Policy Search](https://apstudents.collegeboard.org/getting-credit-placement/search-policies)
2. Note minimum scores for each AP subject you plan to take
3. Use [score calculators](/tools/ap-score-calculator) to set realistic targets
4. Prioritize exams where a 4+ saves the most tuition
