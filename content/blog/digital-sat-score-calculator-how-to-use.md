After every Bluebook practice test, the first question students ask on r/SAT is: **"What would this score be on the real test?"** Our [Digital SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) answers that using module-level correct counts — the same granularity adaptive scoring uses.

## What You Need Before You Start

From a completed Bluebook practice test, gather:

| Module | Max questions | Your correct count |
|---|---|---|
| Reading & Writing Module 1 | 27 | ? |
| Reading & Writing Module 2 | 27 | ? |
| Math Module 1 | 22 | ? |
| Math Module 2 | 22 | ? |

Also note whether each Module 2 felt **easier or harder** than Module 1 — this determines your adaptive path.

## Step 1: Enter Module Correct Counts

Open the [SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) and slide each module to your correct count. The calculator shows:

- **Reading & Writing scaled** (200–800)
- **Math scaled** (200–800)
- **Total score** (400–1600)
- **Approximate national percentile**

## Step 2: Set Module 2 Difficulty

Toggle each section's Module 2 path:

- **Hard path:** You answered enough Module 1 questions correctly to receive the harder Module 2. This unlocks a higher score ceiling (+30 points estimate in our model).
- **Easy path:** Module 2 felt noticeably easier — your Module 1 performance did not qualify for the hard route.

Unsure which path you got? Compare your Module 2 difficulty to Module 1. If Module 2 questions felt similar or harder, select **hard**. Read [how adaptive testing works](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works) for routing thresholds.

## Step 3: Interpret Your Estimate

The calculator uses a linear scaling model aligned with College Board practice test conversion tables. Important caveats:

- **IRT equating varies** by test form — two students with identical raw counts can differ by 10–30 points on different administrations.
- **Hard Module 2 bonus** is an approximation; official routing affects item difficulty, not just a flat bonus.
- Scores are **estimates only** — use them for trend tracking, not college list decisions alone.

## Example Walkthrough

Suppose you scored:

- R&W M1: 20/27, R&W M2: 18/27 (hard path)
- Math M1: 15/22, Math M2: 14/22 (hard path)

Enter these values, set both Module 2 toggles to **hard**, and the calculator estimates your section and total scores. Compare to Bluebook's reported score if available — they should be within ~20 points.

## What to Do After Calculating

1. Identify your weakest module (usually Math M1 or R&W inference questions).
2. Drill that domain using the [Question Bank](/blog/free-official-digital-sat-practice-resources).
3. Retake a full test and recalculate to track improvement.

For score context, see [SAT score percentiles](/blog/sat-score-percentiles-2026) and [is 1200/1300/1400 a good score?](/blog/is-1200-1300-1400-a-good-sat-score).
