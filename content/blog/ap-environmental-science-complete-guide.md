The 2026 AP Environmental Science exam is **Friday, May 15, 2026 (8 AM local)**. Interdisciplinary: biology, earth science, chemistry, and policy.

## Exam Structure

Fully digital — **2 hours 40 minutes**.

| Section | Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section I (MCQ) | 80 | 60% |
| Section II (FRQ) | 3 (10 pts each) | 40% |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |

| 1 | Ecosystems | 6–8% |
| 2 | Biodiversity | 6–8% |

| 4 | Earth Systems and Resources | 10–15% |
| 5 | Land and Water Use | 10–15% |
| 6 | Energy Resources | 10–15% |
| 7 | Atmospheric Pollution | 7–10% |
| 8 | Aquatic/Terrestrial Pollution | 7–10% |
| 9 | Global Change | 15–20% |

**Unit 9 (Global Change)** is the heaviest single unit. Units 3–6 together dominate.

## FRQ Strategy

See also: [APES FRQ guide](/blog/ap-environmental-science-frq-guide)

1. **Design an Investigation** — hypothesis, variables, control, procedure.
2. **Analyze a Problem** — identify cause/effect, propose solution with tradeoffs.
3. **Analyze with Calculation** — show math with units; interpret in environmental context.

**Weeks 1–3:** Units 3–6 (populations through energy).
