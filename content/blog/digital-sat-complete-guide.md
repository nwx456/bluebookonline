**Short answer:** The Digital SAT is ~2 hours 14 minutes — 54 Reading & Writing questions (64 min) plus 44 Math questions (70 min), adaptive Module 2, scored 400–1600.

The **Digital SAT** replaced the paper SAT in the United States and internationally. If you are preparing for 2026 test dates, this guide covers the verified format, adaptive scoring logic, timing, and the free official resources every student should use before test day.

> **Start here:** Use our [Digital SAT score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) after any Bluebook practice test to estimate your 400–1600 score from module-level results.

## Digital SAT Structure at a Glance

The exam has two scored sections, each split into two timed modules:

| Section | Modules | Questions per module | Time per module |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reading & Writing | 2 | 27 | 32 min |
| Math | 2 | 22 | 35 min |

There is a **10-minute break** between sections. You cannot return to Reading & Writing once Math begins.

Total scaled score: **400–1600** (Reading & Writing 200–800 + Math 200–800).

## Adaptive Module 2 Routing

Each section uses **multistage adaptive testing (MST)**:

1. **Module 1** is the same difficulty for all students in a section.
2. Your Module 1 performance determines whether Module 2 is **easier** or **harder**.
3. The **hard Module 2 path** unlocks a higher score ceiling.

This is the single most misunderstood part of Digital SAT prep. Strong Module 1 performance is not optional — it determines whether you can reach top scores. Read our deep dive: [How Digital SAT adaptive testing works](/blog/how-digital-sat-adaptive-testing-works).

## Reading & Writing vs Math

- **Reading & Writing** tests grammar, rhetoric, vocabulary-in-context, and data interpretation across short passages. See the [Reading & Writing complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-reading-writing-complete-guide).
- **Math** includes multiple-choice and student-produced response (grid-in) questions. A **Desmos graphing calculator** is built into Bluebook for the entire Math section. See the [Math complete guide](/blog/digital-sat-math-complete-guide).

## Scoring: No Penalty for Wrong Answers

The Digital SAT uses **rights-only scoring** within each module — there is no guessing penalty. Blank answers and wrong answers both count as incorrect.

Scaled scores are produced through **Item Response Theory (IRT)** and annual equating. Your raw correct count from a practice test is a useful estimate, but official cutoffs vary slightly by test form. Use our [score calculator](/tools/sat-score-calculator) for estimates, not guarantees.

## Key Differences from the Old Paper SAT

| Feature | Paper SAT (pre-2024) | Digital SAT |
|---|---|---|
| Length | ~3 hours | ~2 hours 14 min |
| Reading passages | Long passages, many questions each | Short passages, one question each |
| Math calculator | Section 3 only (no calc) + Section 4 (calc) | Calculator entire Math section |
| Adaptive | No | Yes (Module 2 routing) |
| Delivery | Paper | Bluebook on laptop/tablet |
