The 2026 AP Physics 2 exam is **Thursday, May 7, 2026 (8 AM local)**. Covers thermodynamics, electricity, magnetism, optics, waves, and modern physics — all algebra-based.

## Exam Structure

Hybrid digital — **3 hours**.

| Section | Questions | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Section I (MCQ) | 40 | 50% |
| Section II (FRQ) | 4 | 50% |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Thermodynamics | 12–18% |
| 2 | Electric Force, Field, Potential | 15–19% |
| 3 | Electric Circuits | 15–19% |
| 4 | Magnetism and EM Induction | 12–18% |
| 5 | Geometric Optics | 12–15% |
| 6 | Waves, Sound, Physical Optics | 12–15% |
| 7 | Modern Physics | 12–15% |

Units 2–4 (electricity and magnetism) together are ~42–56% — prioritize circuit analysis and Gauss's-law-style reasoning.

## FRQ Tips

- Draw **circuit diagrams** and label known/unknown quantities.
- For optics: ray diagrams for mirrors and lenses.
- For modern physics: explain photoelectric effect and nuclear decay qualitatively before calculating.

## Study Plan

**Weeks 1–2:** Units 2–3 (E&M fundamentals + circuits).

**Weeks 3–4:** Unit 4 (magnetism) + Unit 1 (thermo).

**Weeks 5–6:** Units 5–7 (optics, waves, modern).
