The 2026 AP Physics C: Mechanics exam is **Wednesday, May 13, 2026 (12 PM local)**. Calculus-based mechanics for future engineers and physics majors.

## Exam Structure

Hybrid digital — **3 hours**. 40 MCQs (50%) + 4 FRQs (50%). Calculator on both sections.

| FRQ | Type | Max Points |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mathematical Routines | 10 |
| 2 | Translation Between Representations | 12 |
| 3 | Experimental Design | 10 |
| 4 | Qualitative/Quantitative Translation | 8 |

## Unit Weights

| Unit | Topic | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Kinematics | 10–15% |
| 2 | Force and Dynamics | 20–30% |
| 3 | Work, Energy, Power | 13–20% |
| 4 | Linear Momentum | 10–18% |
| 5 | Torque and Rotational Dynamics | 10–18% |
| 6 | Rotating Systems | 10–15% |
| 7 | Oscillations | 5–10% |

Unit 2 (dynamics) is the single heaviest unit. Integrate calculus: \(a = dv/dt\), \(W = \int F \cdot dx\).

## Study Plan

Master **differential equations for motion**, **rotational kinematics with \(\alpha\)**, and **energy in non-inertial contexts**. Use released 2025 scoring guidelines from AP Central.
